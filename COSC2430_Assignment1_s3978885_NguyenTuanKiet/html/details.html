About the Book

Author: Miguel de Cervantes Saavedra

Publication Date: 1605

Genre: Adventure

Description


Don Quijote, also known as Don Quixote, is a Spanish novel written by Miguel de Cervantes and originally published in two parts in 1605 and 1615.

The novel tells the story of a middle-aged gentleman named Alonso Quixano who becomes obsessed with the chivalric ideals he has read in books and decides to become a knight-errant himself, taking up his lance and sword to defend the helpless and fight against injustice.

The novel is considered a prototype of the modern novel in part because of its inclusion of many differing viewpoints, which is an early instance of heteroglossia or "multiple voices".

Don Quixote is often cited as one of the greatest novels in world literature and is considered to be the first modern novel.

It has been translated into numerous languages and has had a significant influence on literature and culture worldwide. In fact, Don Quixote is often cited as one of the greatest works of fiction ever written.
